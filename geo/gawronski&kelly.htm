This is an exhibition of installation, objects and sound responding to contemporary manifestations of the modular. In fact the term modular has become intimately associated with figures like 'Le Corbusier', who longed to redesign the world according to principles of purity and utility. Today Corbusier's utopian fantasy although theoretically discredited has made its effects felt in a multiplicity of ways. From modern apartment living to tupperware the world in which we live constantly seeks solutions to problems of time and space. Fixation with the modular reached disturbing heights during the 1950's. The birth of the American (and Australian) Dream imagined a new-Enlightenment in which the commodity would simplify and ultimately satisfy the needs of all those who subscribed to its ideal. Soon however came some rather embarrassing revelations of the restrictive and exploitative effects of commodity fetishism. The modular metamorphosed again. During the 1960's under a tidal wave of alternative marketing, the modular became 'groovy'. This was the era of such items as the home entertainment system and the bubble chair. These were 'developed' further into the 1970's resulting in such must-haves as the double-bed alarm clock radio, the coffee percolator and pre-fab housing. Today metropolises burgeon with ever new modular apartments that promise freedom and lifestyles with street-cred you can buy. 'Modulor' the exhibition is an idiosyncratic exploration of such considerations. It draws analogies between our broader living environments and the things we choose to have in them. Similarly it asks; 'Who saves the space in which our space-savers came?' 'If modular apartments tend toward miniaturization then why not build them for animals or even insects?' 'Why replace commodities if you can simply rotate them?' This exhibition displaces popular notions of modular convenience whilst drawing on its many forms.

Alex Gawronski and Lisa Kelly are co-founders of Sydney artist-run gallery Blaugrau (july 2000-july 2001). Recent joint projects include 'Dead Freight' at Imperial Slacks, Sydney, & 'Arkitekt' at Penthouse & Pavement, Melbourne. Alex's recent exhibitions include 'Primavera' at the Museum of Contemporary Art, Sydney and 'Real Danger' at The Physics Room, Christchurch. Lisa's recent exhibitions include 'Plant Room' at gallery 4A, Sydney. Lisa is also coordinator of 'Uniglory' a limited edition journal of contemporary art and ideas.
