FUGUE 18th - 29th SEPT 2001

 
Georgiana Morison

 

"Somewhere in social space, somewhen, someone is walking….." Paul D. Miller)

 

Fugue is an installation work of video, sound and electronic devices. Fugue explores notions of space as mobile and liquid alongside desires we have to move towards very particular spaces in our hunger for structure. In psychological terms 'fugue' is defined by a loss of memory or identity, especially as a reaction to shock or emotional distress. In extreme cases the experience of losing ones bearings can be disturbing enough to produce an amnesic state

 

A fugue in musical terms, refers in part to the subject being taken up successively by others and developed by their interweaving. Morison combines a Programme Logic Controller with fog machines, microphones and sensor pad activated recorded sounds and lights. As the viewer/ participant moves through the gallery space they simultaneously activate events and sounds. While negoiating the dissolving architecture of the gallery the sound of their movement and of others engagement in the space, is played back through a delay system and in a "fugue".

"Fugue is an experience in disorientation-a mucking around with the mental dimension - a flip or regressive trip or something else that propels movements in unknown directions."

Fugue reminds us that the spaces we occupy are a murky geography of chance encounters and ever shifting connections.

Georgiana Morison is an artist living in Dunedin. She currently works as a part time technician and part time lecturer at Otago Polytechnic School of Art, and exhibits regularly in Dunedin. Morison is a member of SULCUS -an arts project group and co-ordinated the LOAD project in Port Chalmers earlier this year.
