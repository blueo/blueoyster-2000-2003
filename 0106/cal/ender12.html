Noughts, Crosses or Tiddlywinks..?
Paul Johns

All artists are sensitive to the reactions of the viewer; when a work is mocked publicly the artist can either take it personally, ignore it, or use it in some way. The work 'Noughts, crosses or tiddlywinks..?' was derived from a response to a painting by Paul Johns exhibited previously; a response of humour to capture and entertain the gazing eye of the newspaper reader. The press is only too aware of the cursory glance that most items receive, and it is by their use of sensationalism, shock or






humour that will capture the viewers attention. Despite the serious intent of the original art work (a work using the divine proportion, or the golden rectangle, as its main unit) it has been viewed by the press (THE DOMINION) as a giant game of noughts and crosses: or to further emphasise the flippant attitude of the writer it could be a giant game of tiddlywinks.
And it could be.
But it wasn't.
Yes --- it is a painting.
