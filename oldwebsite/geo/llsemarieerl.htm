Les Objets du Desir

Ilse Marie Erl

AUG 7th -18th 2001

As a jeweller, Ilse-Marie Erl is interested in issues of craft practice,body adornment and fetishism. Her show 'Les Objets du Desirí is a contemplation of displacement, categorisation and contextualisation of artefacts within the museum environment. This room installation reflects Erlís nvestigation of the origin and history of the fetishí in its anthropological context. Erl has referenced accounts of fetishism by Freud and Marx, and philosophical theories about the object and object relations. In creating her work from human hair Erl treated hair as a commodity. She advertised and bartered or hair and established a considerable human hair collection through ritualised exchanges of hand made silver tokens. Her investigation into the social understanding of hair began when a friend gave her a beautiful blonde plait of hair that his ex-lover had sent him after their relationship ended. This incident and people's intense reaction to the story, inspired Erl to delve into the psychological theories, value systems and cultural customs surrounding hair. Ilse-Marie Erl hopes to stretch the boundaries and accepted notions of the role of craft practice. In particular, she hopes to challenge common assumptions about the nature and role of jewellery.

Ilse-Marie Erl was born 1961 in Germany and has been living in Titirangi,Auckland since 1993.She studied at Unitec, Faculty of Architecture and Design, majoring inJewellery and graduated 1998 with a Bachelor in 3D Design.In 1999 she received a Creative New Zealand Grant for the publication of her artist book ëLes Objets du Desirí which is part of her show ëLes Objets du Desirí. ëLes Objets du Desirí was exhibited at Lopdell House Gallery, Auckland in 1999 and at the Hawkeís Bay Museum, Napier in 2000. Further venues for the show will be the Sarjeant Gallery, Wanganui and the Dowse Art Museum, Lower Hutt. At present Ilse-Marie Erl works as a tutor in the Jewellery Department and as a lecturer in Critical Studies, Faculty of Architecture and Design,Unitec, Auckland.
