PASTEL PRICK

 

Geoff Heath

OCT 2nd - 13th 2001
 

'upside down boy you're turning me inside out and round and round.'

It was the early 1980s. I was 17 and hanging out at Backstage an underground gay club in Auckland city. I wore tight jeans,drank rum n' coke and danced to Diana Ross and Grace Jones. Back then things weren't so liberal and we were always on edge waiting for the cops to raid the place and drag out all the underagers and anyone else they didn't like the look of. It was also before aids hit so there were plenty of one night stands. I was lookingfor love in all the wrong places.There was an abundace of hangovers,regrets andmissing socks. Pastel Prick is both a response to Robert Maplethorpe's sexually explicit photographs from the 1980's and a reference to the suburban, dustfree bedrooms of my childhood.Pastel Prick kicks sand in the face of sexual guilt. Incorporating elements of childsplay creates both an underlying sexual tension while attempting to reconcile sex with innocence.
