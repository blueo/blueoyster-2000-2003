IMAGES FROM THE ROTTING ARCHIVE JULY10th-21st 2001
 

 
 

 

NATHAN THOMPSON

A haven beneath whose translucent floor the tremulous stars sparkle unfathomably, around which solid vapours briefly congeal inscribing our temporary existance. Based on water in different states arranged into the sky.

"Images from the rotting archive" looks at the fading and corruption of history and memory. The work explores entropy and decay, ghosts, and the things that humans have abandoned.The works present a deserted archaeology through a grouping of images that are collected together for some motive unclear. The archive as rubble, residue and the tailings left behind by human activity. It seems that someone has begun to dig, then moved on. Now the evidence of activity is eroding.
