STORAGE D AUG 21st - 1st Sept 2001

 

Annie Bradley

  
 
" The being I am waiting for is not real. like the mothers breast for the infant, I create and recreate it over and over; and if the other does not come I hallucinate the other : waiting is a delirium."
- Roland Barthes
  
 
 
 
 
 
 
Traces made by woodworms in the floor (read as a musical score ) were the starting point for this composition. Part of the piece was recorded on a harpsichord, with Hemione Johnson improvising while Annie vibrated the strings with a "stimulator" ( a toying with notions of unrequited desire ). The voice part of the work is a man reading the numbers of entropy at the Auckland museum and relates to the desire to master space through counting. The artist draws on Kristeva's formulation of 'monumental time' as a waiting period, an anxiety that she attempts to inhabit in her work
  
Inspired by Lacan's notion of point de capiton [ upholstery stud ], whereby the subject makes sense of language by anchoring signifiers, involving the fabrication of speakers in place of upholstery studs.The sculptures are negative casts, fleshy and metallic, the buttons become like craters, a tremulous skin. Repetition is used in the sound work to achieve a sense of cyclical time "like the mothers breast for the infant."
  
A wiggling ear 'anchors' this part of the work. The ear uses its ancient muscles to move, to hear.....The ancestors, searching for origins; missing information and the unimaginable horror of family relation and genetic similarity. Genealogists talk about a 'non-paternity-event' which occurs a woman has an extra marital conception and has a child which she brings up as though her husband or partner was the father. In reference to these kinds of secrets, the video work uses the edges of micrographic records as a metaphor for those spaces our bodies dwell that are not recorded "officially." She is interested in knowledge systems and the way bodies and experience rupture what can be known or analysed in objective terms
