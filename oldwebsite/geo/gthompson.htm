Grant Thompson

NOV 13th - 24th 2001

  

 
 
Thompson gathered men's white shirts from Dunedin's opportunity shops to produce the work Surfacing, shown at the Dunedin Public Art Gallery earlier this year and again, in the new work, he utilises the discarded traces of human activity to construct his investigations. Thompson isolates each of his chosen objects (sheets, buttons and saucers) from its familiar context and function and then employs them as materials in the complex decorative surfaces he constructs. The works make large the small repetitive daily encounters we have with objects and offer the viewer both sensuous and cerebral opportunities to engage with the surfaces of the everyday.
