Works on Paper
David Hatcher

David Hatcher’s recent reworkings of the visual languages of late capitalism see cold hard economic performance data 'doodled’ into a conflation of jittery market dynamics and associated psychological terrains. Angsty portraiture and a suite of works referencing the 'blotter art' of the psychedelic community investigate 'a kind of contemporary sublime in which economic space obsoletes geographic space as something to be transfixed by'.



Resembling 'Julian Opie on a bad trip vs On Kawara on an even worse one', the works consider 'hallucinogenic geopolitical conditions underpinning contemporary notions of collective identity in the developed world'.
David Hatcher lives and works in Puhoi and Berlin.
